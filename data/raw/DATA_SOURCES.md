# Data Sources for Poverty Point Project

## Chronological Data

### Radiocarbon Dating
Primary source: [Kidder et al. 2021](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/southeastern-archaeology) and related publications

**Key chronological findings:**
- Ridge construction: 1600-1300 BCE (based on radiocarbon)
- Mound A construction: Rapid, possibly within 90 days (Ortmann & Kidder 2013)
- Ridge West 3: Exceptionally rapid construction (Kidder et al. 2021)
- Burning preceding Mound A: 1450-1250 BCE

**Recent revisions:**
- Jaketown site may be earlier than Poverty Point itself
- Cedarland occupied ~500 years before Poverty Point
- Challenges radial diffusion model of Poverty Point influence

### Bayesian Modeling
Source: [Chronological Hygiene paper in American Antiquity](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-antiquity/article/chronological-hygiene-and-bayesian-modeling-of-poverty-point-sites-in-the-lower-mississippi-valley-circa-4200-to-3200-cal-bp/E00DB4D0A722C508597D24B47F33660F)

## Construction Data

### Labor Estimates
Source: Ortmann & Kidder 2013 (*Geoarchaeology*)

| Feature | Volume (m³) | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Mound A | 238,500 | Built in ≤90 days |
| Total ridges | ~750,000 | 6 concentric C-shapes |
| Total earthwork | ~1,000,000 | All features combined |

### Construction Chronology
- Multiple construction episodes identified
- Ridge construction spans 1600-1300 BCE
- Evidence of planned, rapid building events
- Not gradual accretion

## Exotic Materials

### Source Distances
| Material | Primary Source | Distance from PP |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| Copper | Great Lakes OR southern Appalachians | 1,000-1,600 km |
| Galena | Potosi, Missouri; Upper Mississippi | 800+ km |
| Steatite | Appalachian foothills | 700+ km |
| Novaculite | Ouachita Mountains | 250 km |
| Burlington chert | Illinois | 800 km |
| Quartz crystal | Arkansas | 300 km |

### Quantities
- Estimated 71 metric tons of exotic materials over ~600 years
- 700+ galena artifacts identified by 1970
- Significant quantities of steatite vessels

### Provenience Analysis
- [Copper LA-ICP-MS analysis](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352409X16300669): Some copper from southern Appalachians
- [Galena trace element analysis](https://pages.vassar.edu/tracing-galena-artifacts-at-poverty-point-back-to-their-source/): Majority from Potosi deposit, Missouri

## Paleoclimate Data

### Challenges
- Southeastern U.S. identified as data-poor region for paleoclimate
- No tree-ring records for this period/region
- Must rely on coarser proxies

### Available Proxies
1. **Sea level data**: Rate slowed to 0.3 mm/yr by ~4.0 ka
2. **Mississippi delta evolution**: St. Bernard subdelta active 3.5-2.5 ka
3. **Gulf sediment cores**: USGS reconstructions available
4. **Regional drought patterns**: Major droughts documented 50 BCE-450 CE and 800-1100 CE

### Relevant Sources
- [USGS Paleoclimate Reconstruction](https://www.usgs.gov/centers/spcmsc/science/paleoclimate-reconstruction-marine-and-lake-sediments)
- [Holocene Synthesis Project](https://usgs.gov/centers/fbgc/science/holocene-synthesis-project)
- [Late Holocene delta evolution](https://esurf.copernicus.org/articles/5/689/2017/)

## Regional Site Data

### Major Sites
1. **Poverty Point (16WC5)**: Type site, ~160 ha
2. **Jaketown, MS**: Earlier than PP, smaller
3. **Claiborne, MS**: Shell ring, coastal trade route
4. **Cedarland, LA**: Occupied ~500 years before PP
5. **J.W. Copes, LA**: Small mound complex

### Site Files
- Louisiana Division of Archaeology maintains state site files
- Mississippi Department of Archives and History
